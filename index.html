Description

As a child, I was fortunate to have attended a blind school where I received support in various aspects of my life such as assistive technology, braille courses and adaptive sports, however, I always lacked the comfort of having people I could talk to, who had the lived experience of a blind or low vision individual. And that is why as a tutor of the visually impaired, I not only offer personalised braille and screen reader training, tailored advice on pathways for the future, and strategies for a more independent daily life, I also offer mentorship. A space of understanding. A place where each and every one of my clients feels heard and validated. Because while blindness might mean lack of sight, it does not mean lack of vision.

What To Expect

Each session will vary from between 30 minutes to 1 hour, depending on the preference of the client.

The first session will begin with a discussion about your current situation, short term and then long term goals as an individual who is blind or has low vision.

We will then discuss how we can work together to implement strategies that will help you reach those goals.

Every session will begin with a check-in of how you've been and what you feel you need from the session. Zoom will be used to carry out sessions, and parents are more than welcome to be a part of these sessions.
